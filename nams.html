NAMs through a Whole‑Organism Lens

New Alternative Methodologies (NAMs) advance research by reducing and replacing animal use where feasible. This guide explains where whole model organisms complement NAMs, and how verification and validation build confidence in translational relevance.

Why a Whole Model for NAMs?

NAMs excel at dissecting mechanisms in defined systems. Whole‑organism models supply integrated physiology, complex interplay among tissues, lifespan effects, and emergent properties not accessible in isolation.

Context, Complexity, and Translation

  • Systemic interactions and homeostasis under real biological constraints
  • Developmental timing, aging, sex, and genetic background effects
  • Pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics in vivo
  • Pathway cross‑talk, compensatory mechanisms, and pleiotropy

Verification of NAMs

Verification asks: does a NAM measure what it claims under defined conditions? This emphasizes internal consistency, technical performance, and fit‑for‑purpose criteria.

Assay fidelity and controls
Ensure well‑characterized controls, dynamic range, sensitivity, specificity, and repeatability within the NAM.
Reference materials and benchmarks
Use reference standards and benchmark datasets to establish expected behavior and detect drift.
Data quality and provenance
Document sample handling, instrument settings, pipelines, and QC metrics to support robust interpretation.
Fit‑for‑purpose criteria
Align NAM outputs with the decision context (screening vs. definitive testing) and define acceptance thresholds.

Validation of NAMs

Validation evaluates whether a NAM’s outputs generalize and predict biological outcomes beyond initial test sets — including in vivo reference points when appropriate.

Comparative and Predictive Evidence

  • Cross‑model concordance: NAM vs. orthogonal NAMs and in vivo endpoints
  • Robustness across labs, batches, and biological diversity
  • Prospective prediction on blinded or out‑of‑distribution samples
  • Uncertainty quantification and error analysis

The Role of Whole Models

Organism‑level results provide ground truth for complex phenotypes, validating the translational relevance of NAMs where systemic interactions matter.

MMRRC Models for NAMs

The MMRRC provides curated, cryo‑archived mouse lines and expertise to verify and validate NAMs where whole‑organism endpoints are needed.

Model Categories (Examples)

  • Disease‑area specific models (e.g., neurodegeneration, metabolic)
  • Reporter and conditional lines
  • Controls and background references
  • Tool strains for pathway interrogation

Best practices for integrating NAMs with whole‑organism data
Define hypotheses spanning reductionist and organismal levels, align endpoints, and pre‑register criteria for concordance and divergence.
Quality and reporting
Provide complete metadata (genetic background, housing, age/sex, health status) and document assay protocols to support reproducibility.
